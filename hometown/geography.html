Geography

Bengaluru boasts a diverse geography of urban landscapes, lush gardens, and surrounding hills

TERRAIN

Bengaluru is situated on the Deccan Plateau, at an elevation of about 3,000 feet above sea level. The plateau terrain contributes to the city's relatively moderate climate and scenic landscapes.

WATER BODIES

The city is dotted with numerous lakes, both natural and man-made. These include iconic water bodies like Ulsoor Lake and Bellandur Lake. Bengaluru's geography has been shaped by these lakes, providing recreational spaces and contributing to the city's water resources.

GREENERY

Bengaluru is known for its green cover, with parks and gardens interspersed throughout the urban expanse. Notable examples include Cubbon Park and Lalbagh Botanical Garden. This emphasis on greenery adds a unique aspect to the city's geography, offering visitors alike pockets of natural beauty.
