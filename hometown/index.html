Bengaluru is also known as

IT Hub

Bengaluru is renowned as a major Information Technology (IT) hub, housing numerous tech parks, software companies, and startups. It plays a crucial role in the global IT industry and is referred to as the Silicon Valley of India.

Garden City

Bengaluru is also called the "Garden City of India" due to its numerous parks and green spaces. Lalbagh Botanical Garden and Cubbon Park are iconic examples, contributing to the city's pleasant and green environment.

Culinary Delight

The city is known for its diverse culinary scene, offering a wide range of cuisines from traditional South Indian dishes to international flavors. Bengaluru has a vibrant food culture, with its numerous cafes, restaurants, and street food options.

HISTORY

Bengaluru, the capital city of the Indian state of Karnataka, has a history that dates back over a thousand years. In the 16th century, the region was part of the Vijayanagara Empire. The foundations of the city were laid when Kempe Gowda I, a local chieftain, established a mud fort in 1537. This fort, known as Bengaluru Pete, marked the beginning of the city.

The late 20th century witnessed a transformative phase for Bengaluru as it emerged as a major hub for information technology. The city's IT boom in the late 20th and early 21st centuries earned it the nickname "Silicon Valley of India." Today, Bengaluru is not only a leading IT hub but also a thriving metropolis known for its cultural diversity, green spaces, and a blend of tradition and modernity.
